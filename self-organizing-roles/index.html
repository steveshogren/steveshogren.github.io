DRAFT :: Why Have Job Titles on a Self-Organizing Team?

20 Feb, 2017 · Read in about 1 min · (186 Words)
management meta game self-organization

My team has a decade of experience applying self-organizing teams and pair-programming to reduce wasted productivity in an enterprise banking application.

Self-correction and self-organization is on a continuum. For example, our developers are not expected to decide what features to work on next. Our team of business experts and analysts think up and prioritize new strategic initiatives. The domain is so complex and our customers are so huge that it would be unreasonable to expect a developer to do as good as a job as our industry experts. While they do take advice from developers and QA staff, ultimately we all agree that role is filled best by someone focusing on it full-time. Since the teams can be fluid, several people have rotated from development to QA to business.

Similarly, while our development staff writes and maintains a huge number of automated tests, our QA staff has a whole second set that they maintain with the business experts. While we all are trusted and allowed to rotate and learn a new role, we all agree having the three roles separate allows us to compartmentalize and focus.
